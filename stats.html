Economic Impact

$8.3 Billion

Estimated annual lifetime economic cost of intimate partner violence in the United States

Source: CDC

8 Million Days

Days of paid work lost annually due to domestic violence—equivalent to 32,000 full-time jobs

Source: CDC

Workplace Impact

96% of employed domestic violence survivors experience problems at work due to abuse. Effects include tardiness, missed work, keeping a job, and job performance.

Source: Corporate Alliance to End Partner Violence

Vulnerable Populations

LGBTQ+ Community

  • 44% of lesbian women and 61% of bisexual women experience rape, physical violence, and/or stalking by an intimate partner
  • 26% of gay men and 37% of bisexual men experience rape, physical violence, and/or stalking by an intimate partner
  • Transgender individuals face disproportionately high rates of domestic violence and discrimination

Source: CDC, NCADV

Native Americans

  • More than 4 in 5 American Indian and Alaska Native women have experienced violence in their lifetime
  • Native American women are murdered at more than 10 times the national average
  • Over half of Native American women have experienced sexual violence

Source: National Institute of Justice, CDC

People with Disabilities

  • Women with disabilities are 40% more likely to experience intimate partner violence than women without disabilities
  • People with disabilities often face unique forms of abuse including withholding medication, destroying assistive devices, or threatening institutionalization

Source: American Psychological Association

Immigrants & Refugees

  • Immigrant women may face additional barriers including language barriers, fear of deportation, lack of knowledge about rights, and cultural isolation
  • Abusers often use immigration status as a tool of control and manipulation

Source: National Immigrant Women's Advocacy Project

Impact on Children

Children who witness domestic violence are at higher risk for:

  • Anxiety, depression, and low self-esteem
  • Behavioral problems and aggression
  • Academic difficulties and poor school performance
  • Becoming victims or perpetrators of violence later in life
  • Physical health problems including asthma, allergies, and gastrointestinal issues
  • Sleep disturbances and nightmares

Source: American Academy of Pediatrics, UNICEF

15.5 Million

Children in the United States live in families where intimate partner violence occurred at least once in the past year

Source: UNICEF

These Numbers Represent Real People

Behind every statistic is a person—a mother, father, sibling, friend, neighbor—who deserves safety, dignity, and support. If you or someone you know is experiencing domestic violence, help is available 24/7.
