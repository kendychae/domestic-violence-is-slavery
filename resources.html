National Resources

National Domestic Violence Hotline

24/7

Phone: 1-800-799-SAFE (7233)

TTY: 1-800-787-3224

Text: Text START to 88788

Website: thehotline.org

Free, confidential support 24/7 in over 200 languages. Trained advocates available to provide crisis intervention, safety planning, and referrals to local resources.

RAINN (Rape, Abuse & Incest National Network)

24/7

Hotline: 1-800-656-HOPE (4673)

Online Chat: hotline.rainn.org/online

Website: rainn.org

National Sexual Assault Hotline providing support for survivors of sexual violence and their loved ones.

National Coalition Against Domestic Violence (NCADV)

Phone: 1-303-839-1852

Website: ncadv.org

Leading organization working to end domestic violence through advocacy, public policy, and community resources.

Love Is Respect (Teen Dating Abuse)

24/7

Phone: 1-866-331-9474

Text: Text LOVEIS to 22522

Online Chat: loveisrespect.org

Specialized resource for teens and young adults facing dating abuse and unhealthy relationships.

National Resource Center on Domestic Violence

Phone: 1-800-537-2238

Website: nrcdv.org

Comprehensive information and tools for advocates, practitioners, and communities addressing domestic violence.

StrongHearts Native Helpline

7am-10pm CT

Phone: 1-844-7NATIVE (762-8483)

Online Chat: strongheartshelpline.org

Culturally-appropriate support for Native Americans and Alaska Natives affected by domestic and sexual violence.

National Center on Domestic Violence, Trauma & Mental Health

Phone: 1-312-726-7020

Website: nationalcenterdvtraumamh.org

Resources addressing the intersection of domestic violence, trauma, and mental health.

988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline

24/7

Dial: 988

Phone: 1-800-273-TALK (8255)

Text: Text 988

Website: 988lifeline.org

Free, confidential crisis support for anyone experiencing suicidal thoughts or emotional distress.

Important Disclaimer

This website is for informational purposes only and is not a substitute for professional help.

If you or someone you know is in immediate danger, please call 911 or your local emergency services. The information provided on this site is intended to be educational and supportive, but it should not replace advice from qualified professionals including law enforcement, medical personnel, therapists, or legal advisors.

We strive to keep all resource links current and accurate, but organizations may change contact information or services. Please verify critical information directly with service providers.

Privacy & Safety: If you're in an unsafe situation, please use private browsing mode and remember to clear your browser history. Use the Quick Exit button at the top of any page to immediately leave this site.
