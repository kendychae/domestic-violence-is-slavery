Understanding Domestic Violence

📖

What Is Domestic Violence?

Domestic violence (also called intimate partner violence, domestic abuse, or relationship abuse) is a pattern of behaviors used by one partner to maintain power and control over another partner in an intimate relationship.

Domestic violence does not discriminate. It affects people of all ages, economic backgrounds, education levels, races, religions, genders, and sexual orientations.

⚠️

Forms of Abuse

  • Physical Abuse: Hitting, slapping, kicking, restraining, or any unwanted physical contact
  • Emotional/Psychological Abuse: Intimidation, manipulation, humiliation, isolation
  • Sexual Abuse: Any non-consensual sexual activity or coercion
  • Economic Abuse: Controlling finances, preventing employment, sabotaging work
  • Digital Abuse: Using technology to harass, stalk, or control
💡

Why This Resource Exists

This website exists to provide accessible, comprehensive, and life-saving information to anyone affected by domestic violence.

Whether you're experiencing abuse, supporting a loved one, or seeking to educate yourself, you'll find:

  • 24/7 crisis hotlines and emergency resources
  • Information about recognizing abuse patterns
  • Statistics and research to understand the scope
  • Local Idaho resources and national support services

Important Disclaimer

This website is for informational purposes only and is not a substitute for professional help.

If you or someone you know is in immediate danger, please call 911 or your local emergency services. The information provided on this site is intended to be educational and supportive, but it should not replace advice from qualified professionals including law enforcement, medical personnel, therapists, or legal advisors.

We strive to keep all resource links current and accurate, but organizations may change contact information or services. Please verify critical information directly with service providers.
